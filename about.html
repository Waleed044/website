Squid Game

Squid Game is a South Korean survival drama television series created by Hwang Dong-hyuk. It premiered on Netflix on September 17, 2021, and became a global phenomenon.

The series revolves around 456 financially desperate contestants who participate in a mysterious survival competition. They compete in a series of children's games, each with a deadly twist. The winner stands to claim a massive cash prize, while failure means eliminationâ€”literally.

The games are hosted by masked guards under the direction of a mysterious "Front Man," with the players unaware of the true stakes until they are already ensnared in the deadly competition. The story is told through the lens of Seong Gi-hun (Player 456), a down-on-his-luck chauffeur and gambler who joins the game in hopes of clearing his debts and helping his family.

Squid Game ignited global discussions about class issues and capitalism. Its symbols (e.g., the shapes, doll, and Dalgona candy) became cultural phenomena, influencing memes, merchandise, and Halloween costumes. It revitalized interest in South Korean media, boosting Netflix's investment in Korean productions.
